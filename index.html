  • Spring 2014

    Ways of Knowing(KNW 2300)

    I Collaborated with a team of 5 engineers to effectively produce a working design for a robot. My job was to help build the sensors for the robot to test water for salinity and turbidity.



    I also used Java in order to collect data from the various sensors and told the robot how to interpret it.

  • Summer 2014

    Intern with CITI Group

    Worked on Mongo DB/Splunk and used SQL and NOSQL databases in order to better organize large amounts of data that we had collected.



    Built sensors with Tanium using shell script to test various endpoints on hundreds of Linux and Windows based virtual machines. I then took that raw data and exported it to excel sheets. Afterwards, I proceeded to write several visual basic programs to better organize the random data produced by the sensors.



    Trained the 7 members on my team on how to use a new tool from CITI to better organize their workflow. This tool was eventually fully adopted and allowed our team to be more transparent and efficient.

  • Winter 2015

    Hack DFW(5th Place)

    Competed with a team of engineers against 800 other students in a 24 hour competition designed to see who could create the best product.



    While at Hack DFW, I worked with a team of engineers with the goal of bulding an app that communicated with a Ilumi light to produce certain colors based on the current weather. As a result, our team placed in 5th overall for best product.



    I also personally built a product that could sense when the temperature got too hot or too cold outside and would alert the user by letting out a warning sound. The purpose of this was to help prevent heat exhaustion for kids who play sports outside. The device could let the coaches know if conditions were too extreme allowing them to either move practice indoors or let the kids have a water break. The results would allow the coaches to push their player while preventing injury.

  • Summer 2015

    Intern with CITI Group

    I was given the opportunity to return for a second internship at CITI where I created a Jython program that pulled data from our Database Automation log files. After I completed this step, the program then takes the data and generates a dynamic HTML web page that shows the overall status of our Database Automation Platform. It also informs the user if there are any issues and will help them pinpoint the exact cause of the problem. Finally, the program will insert and index the data into a MongoDB database in order to have a record of all past status reports.



    I completed M101P(MongoDB for Developers - Python) and M102P(Mongo for DBAS) which are courses offered by MongoDB and covers topics like CRUD, schema design, indexing, aggregation framework, replication, scalability, and backup and recovery.
